# The Profound Meaning of Constant Tranquility

## The Eight Thieves and the Ten Losses

All people in this world are harmed by these six thieves, seven emotions, and ten losses. I advise all good people to eliminate these six thieves, seven emotions, and ten losses completely, return to the right path, and never board the ship of thieves, lest they fall into the abyss and regret it later.

The Poem of Wuxin Daoren (Daoist) says:

> The eyes do not observe colors, the nose does not smell fragrances.  
> With a sincere and righteous mind, one preserves the true nature.  
> The three realms are empty, there is nothing.  
> No birth, no death, the lifespan is eternal.

The Poem of Yin Zhenren (Daoist) says:

> The spiritual light shines on the River Ganges day and night.  
> Saints and mortals originally share the same family.  
> When the mind is free from thoughts, the whole being manifests.  
> As soon as the six senses move, they are obscured by clouds.

## The Explanation of Constant Tranquility by the Highest Venerable Lord, Chapter 9: On Temperament

The reason one cannot [expel desires] is that the mind is not yet clarified, and desires are not yet expelled.

The mind is not yet clarified, and desires are not yet expelled. This is an inability to sweep away the three minds and four appearances.

"The mind is not yet clarified" means this person's mind is not yet dead.
"Desires are not yet expelled" means the seven emotions and six desires have not yet departed.
Therefore, they cannot become immortal, become a Buddha, become a sage, or become a worthy. 

Why is this so? It is clear that they cannot let go of joy, anger, sorrow, and happiness.

If one can truly let go of the emotion of joy and transform it into original nature, let go of the emotion of anger and transform it into original emotion, let go of the emotion of happiness and transform it into original spirit, let go of the emotion of sorrow and transform it into original essence, and let go of desires and transform them into the five elements, what immortal could one not become? What Buddha could one not attain?

The Confucians say: "Be cautious of what you do not see, be fearful of what you do not hear." The Buddhists say: "No eyes, ears, nose, tongue, body, or mind. No color, sound, smell, taste, touch, or dharma." The Daoists say: "Indistinct, indistinct, dark, dark." If one follows the practices of the Three Teachings' sages and saints, what private matter could one not let go of? What desire could one not expel?

The sages of the Three Teachings all teach people to let go of their private desires, for private desires belong to the yin. The sages of the Three Teachings all teach people to preserve their heavenly principles, for heavenly principles belong to the yang.

## The Explanation of Constant Tranquility by the Highest Venerable Lord, Chapter 10: On Emptiness

Ones who can expel [desires] observe their mind inwardly, finding the mind to be without mind. They observe their form outwardly, finding the form to be without form. They observe objects from afar, finding objects to be without objects. 

The three having been realized, only emptiness is seen.

To expel [desires] is to drive away all distracting thoughts to other realms. To observe the mind inwardly is to close the eyes and look within. "The mind is without mind" means that thoughts arise from the mind, but the mind itself is gone. See where the thoughts come from. 

To observe the form outwardly is to close the eyes and look outside. "The form is without form" means that the mind arises from the form, but the form itself is gone. See where the mind comes from.

To observe objects from afar is to close the eyes and look far into the heavens, earth, sun, moon, and stars - all are gone. See where the body comes from.

The three having been realized, it means that the mind, body, and objects all seem to be gone. "Only emptiness is seen" means that the Three Realms of heaven, earth, and humanity, and the myriad things, do not have a single thing. It is a dark, obscure void. Only eternal emptiness remains unresolved.
